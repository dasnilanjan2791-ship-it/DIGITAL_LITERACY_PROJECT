Digital literacy :

refers to the ability to use digital technologies effectively for learning, communication, and problem-solving. It includes skills such as evaluating online information, adapting to new tools, and practicing responsible digital behavior.
 
For students, useful digital tools: 

include productivity platforms like Google Workspace and Microsoft Office, collaboration apps such as Zoom and Microsoft Teams, research resources like Google Scholar and Mendeley, and organizational tools such as Trello or Evernote.

 Safe internet practices:

 are essential and involve using strong, unique passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, avoiding suspicious links, keeping software updated, and being cautious about sharing personal information. Building a professional online presence means maintaining updated LinkedIn or academic profiles, showcasing achievements thoughtfully, and engaging respectfully in online communities while avoiding content that could harm one’s reputation.

 Finally, email etiquette:

 requires clear subject lines, formal greetings, concise and professional language, careful proofreading, and timely, polite responses to ensure effective and respectful communication.
